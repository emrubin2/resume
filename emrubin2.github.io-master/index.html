Evan Rubin




Introduction
Hey, I’m Evan! Welcome to my portfolio. I would like to introduce myself as you check out my work. I was raised in rural south-central Pennsylvania which gave me a fondness for visiting cities. I have also had the recent pleasure of visiting family in Bolivia and cruising to the Bahamas. When not traveling, I’m more of a homebody. I like to go for jogs around my quiet neighborhood, enjoy a robust craft beer at a local brewery, and love to try the latest restaurants. I am passionate about using geographic data to help populations through various methods like urban planning, rural farming, and the military, I have done it all. It is so intriguing the way that the same methods can be applied in a variety of formats to continue to help people. This is my underlying value and the reason I continue in this work. Thank you for your time and interest.

My interest in GIS and computer science began in 2005 with the creation of Google Street View and Google Earth. At the age of 12, I was fascinated with this new technology and grew a deep interest in all the intricate work involved. After graduating high school, I began at the local college to gain two degrees in Geography (Geospatial Applications concentration) and Criminology. I graduated from Millersville University of Pennsylvania in 2015 with a wealth of knowledge and the passion to begin in the GIS field. I began my working career with Trimble, as a GIS Technician here I aided in the development of their GPS applications. In 2018, I transitioned into a GIS Specialist position with the Department of Public Works at Aberdeen Proving Grounds military base, where I had the pleasure of serving alongside our nation’s military in mapping community plans. Simultaneously, I began my graduate studies in GIS and Web Map Development at the University of Wisconsin. The following year, while continuing my studies, I started a new role as the GIS Administrator for the City of Harrisburg. It is here that really began to utilize the tools provided by ESRI to create web maps and applications. I graduated from Wisconsin in 2020 and plan to continue working in a field for which I have held a deep passion since my youth.

Short Term Career Goals
Upon my graduation, I hope to maintain utilizing the diverse skills learned throughout my master’s program. Additionally, using the standing of having a master’s degree to take more of a leadership role either at the City of Harrisburg or at another local business.

Long Term Career Goals
Looking ahead, I would greatly enjoy utilizing the skills learned throughout a career in various GIS roles to work in urban planning or to branch out and do contract research work. After holding a variety of jobs to really hone my GIS skills, I feel there will be a point in my career where I will be well versed in all the essentials of the field to be a successful contract employee. This will allow the job to remain different and interesting rather than growing stale as well as helping people of a variety of backgrounds.
